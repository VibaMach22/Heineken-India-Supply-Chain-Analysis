# 🍺 Heineken India — Supply Chain & Regulatory Impact Analysis

> **How does state-level regulatory fragmentation affect UBL/Heineken's 
> supply chain cost and distribution efficiency across India?**

---

## 📌 Project Overview

United Breweries Limited (UBL), the Indian subsidiary of Heineken N.V., 
commands over 50% of India's formal beer market. Yet despite its scale, 
UBL operates in one of the most complex regulatory environments of any 
consumer goods company in the world — where each of India's 28+ states 
controls its own excise duty, pricing approvals, distribution licenses, 
and interstate movement rules.

This project analyses how that state-level fragmentation creates 
measurable supply chain pressure points — and how UBL is strategically 
responding through cold chain investment, brewery expansion, and 
digital infrastructure.

**This is not a financial analysis. This is an operations and 
supply chain intelligence report built from public data.**

---

## 🔍 Central Research Question

*How does India's state-level alcohol excise fragmentation impact 
UBL/Heineken's distribution cost structure, inventory positioning, 
and supply chain resilience — and where are the highest-pressure markets?*

## 🧠 Key Findings (Summary)

> *Full analysis in `/reports` — this is a high-level snapshot*

1. **The Tax Break Problem** — Excise duty varies from 28% (Maharashtra) 
   to 65% (Bihar), meaning the same SKU can have a near-2x retail price 
   difference across states. This forces UBL into state-specific pricing 
   and inventory strategies that add significant operational overhead.

2. **The Telangana Shutdown Case** — In January 2025, UBL suspended all 
   beer supply to Telangana (pop. 40M) after the state refused price 
   revision requests. This is a live example of how regulatory friction 
   can trigger full supply chain disruption — not demand collapse, 
   not logistics failure, but pure policy risk.

3. **Cold Chain as Competitive Moat** — UBL's visi cooler deployment 
   (branded refrigerators at retail point-of-sale) is outpacing 
   AB InBev and Carlsberg. In India's climate, cold availability at 
   point-of-sale directly drives impulse purchase conversion. 
   This is supply chain strategy functioning as marketing.

4. **The Northern Gap** — UBL's brewery network is historically 
   concentrated in South India. The ₹750 crore greenfield brewery 
   in Unnao, Uttar Pradesh (target: Q2 FY27) directly addresses 
   the supply chain inefficiency of serving northern demand 
   from southern production capacity.

5. **Broken Input Tax Chain** — UBL pays GST on raw materials 
   (barley, hops, aluminium packaging) but cannot offset this against 
   state excise on the final product. Packaging alone is ~40% of 
   cost structure. This creates structural margin compression 
   that competitors face equally — but scale gives UBL more 
   resilience than smaller players.
